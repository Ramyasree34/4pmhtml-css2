"Breaking Free from Plastic: The Battle for a Cleaner Planet"

Plastic pollution is the widespread contamination of the environment with plastic waste, which poses a significant threat to ecosystems and wildlife. Plastics, especially single-use items like bottles, bags, and packaging, take hundreds of years to decompose, accumulating in oceans, rivers, and landscapes.

Marine animals can ingest or become entangled in plastics, leading to injury or death. This pollution also impacts human health as microplastics enter the food chain. Reducing plastic use, recycling, and promoting sustainable alternatives are key steps toward addressing this growing environmental crisis.
